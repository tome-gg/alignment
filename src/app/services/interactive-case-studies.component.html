Interactive Case Studies

Role play. Nightmare scenarios. Unexpected situations.

Who benefits the most from interactive case studies?

  • People who have strong fundamental knowledge, but want to test their skills in a real-time, adaptive environment.
  • People who want to learn software architecture and understand each and every flow of data and systems.
  • People who want to learn strategies on how to diagnose and debug systems effectively and efficiently.
  • People who want to learn about security and optimization failures.

What kinds of results does the interactive case studies achieve?

  • You learn to ask better questions.
  • You learn to problem solve better, by focusing more on direction, strategy, and tactics rather than execution (e.g. typing, navigating the portal in your cloud provider, reading documentation, trial and error programming).
  • You learn to recognize and be comfortable admitting when you don't know the answer.
