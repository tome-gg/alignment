Tome.gg Value Proposition

Our coaching and mentoring services advance you in these three areas.

Communication

Software engineers are excellent when it comes to deciphering the errors and problems that the machine spits out, and navigating Google to further understand the problem so that they can instruct the machine to do as required.

Unfortunately, majority of engineers struggle to express themselves accurately and concisely, and sometimes have some anxieties when it comes to speaking.

Tome.gg mentors address this by training you to write and speak accurately and in an intellectually humble manner.

Best practices in growth

Imposter syndrome. It's when you feel like you're not good enough and that you've faked your way into the higher role that you currently have, and you're anxiously waiting for the moment you are discovered, thrown out, or replaced.

This is a symptom commonly experienced by software engineers at various levels: from junior engineers way up to the executive levels such as CTOs and engineering managers.

Tome.gg mentors address this by helping you see your growth and progress accurately, by helping you pay attention to your true performance and identify bad growth habits that hinder you from accomplishing your majestic goals.

Professional contexts

You may have grown and performed exceptionally well and now your CTO asks you to deal with junior developers, managing and leading people. Simply, transform your 10x developer performance into 1000x by teaching and mentoring.

Not everyone is equipped to teach, lead, mentor, or manage people. That's why we've collected resources and written operational guides on the different professional contexts such as code reviews, performance reviews, sprint planning, and more.

Tome.gg mentors accelerate you in navigating these scenarios and situations so that you won't need to spend 12 months experimenting and discovering that a set of methods don't work. Review our catalog, and analyze which context works best for you.

Tome.gg Products and Services

Own your growth journal and immerse yourself in case studies.

Data ownership

Your growth data isn't meant to be warehoused in some foreign company or lost in the Google Workspace of the company you just left. Why should you be prevented from your growth data, including both your struggles and perseverance in your growth and your mentor's and advisor's feedback?

The value of your growth is only coincidentally useful to the company or the school that you are with as long as you are with them. Afterwards, your growth data is no longer valuable to them, but it is a huge loss for you.

Your knowledge is the output of your growth. But your journey towards it-- that growth data is yours. It is your asset, and it is everyone's first asset. Ownership of your growth is one of the first steps towards financial freedom, sustainability, and self-actualization.

This is why we developed the Tome.gg protocols: to teach others how to own and protect their data in the form of your own personal growth journal.

Growth Journal

Tome.gg mentors guide you with the best practices in growth, both in theoretical (mindsets, idealogies, strategies, and direction) and in the applied sense (actual training, guidance, feedback, accountability).

Our coaching and mentoring programs aren't designed for you to be dependent on us. In fact, our mission is to enable hundreds of individuals to become self-sustaining, independent lifelong learners that compound their growth and accelerates and uplifts others. Our job is completed when you and your mentor have exhausted most if not all of the skills you can acquire from your mentor.

In Tome.gg, mentors guide you in how to write about your growth data following the Tome.gg protocol at a repository that you personally own, which we do not have access to. You decide who to grant read or write access to it. The founder of Tome.gg practices this learn-in-public writing of their growth to demonstrate and inspire others to also take ownership and responsibility of their personal growth.
