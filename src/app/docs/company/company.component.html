Limited communication and negotiation vocabulary.

I've met a lot of highly-proficient, deeply-technical software engineers that have struggled to understand how to better deliver value to their direct supervisors, CTOs, or the company. They also lack the familiarity in dealing with negotiations escalating to "new-job-interview-then-quit" approaches which discard all of their accumulated non-monetary assets like team reputation and influence, and strong relationships.

Personalization.

Throughout my journey in my 18+ years of programming and software engineering, I've learned what works best for me when it comes to learning.

I've been teaching subjects (math, science, computer science) for over 15+ years in a variety of different roles and one of the keys to effective learning is personalization: in goals, in learning strategies and tactics, in communication styles and preferences, in method of instruction, in pace, and in content.

How does one effectively discover these personalization dimensions of mentorship and learning?

We looked for the answers and collected it: the Tome.gg Protocols. It's free. Then we built a coaching and mentorship program for people who want to work with the best mentors to help them accelerate quickly.
